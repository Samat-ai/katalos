# Katalos demo checklist (2–3 minutes)

Before recording, prepare a signed-in owner with a profile username, at least one public entry in each of the finished, in-progress, planned, and abandoned states, and an OpenAI key configured. Keep one private entry available to verify that the public room does not reveal it.

1. **0:00–0:20 — Land in the room.** Open the home page and introduce Katalos: a shared media room that makes reading and watching taste explorable. Point out the two labeled spaces: the reading nook for books and manga, and the TV nook for anime and movies.
2. **0:20–0:50 — Read the room at a glance.** Highlight a finished cover on the shelf/cabinet, a current item near the reader or in the TV player, and an abandoned item in its pile. Explain that the status grouping updates from one shared media-entry model.
3. **0:50–1:20 — Add an item.** Open `/room`, choose **Add media**, fill in a title, media type, status, synopsis, optional rating/note, and public visibility, then save. Point to the new cover in the correct room zone. Briefly mention that edit and delete controls are available below the room.
4. **1:20–1:45 — Share safely.** Open `/u/<username>` in an anonymous/private browser window. Show that this is a read-only public room and confirm the private test entry is not shown.
5. **1:45–2:05 — Inspect an item.** Tap/click a cover and show the accessible detail drawer with type, status, synopsis, rating, and note. Close it with the labeled close button.
6. **2:05–2:30 — Taste Profiler moment.** Select **Read my taste**, wait for the grounded, spoiler-safe response, and show its archetype, three signals, and first-pick recommendation. State that it is generated from public room entries only.
7. **2:30–2:45 — Close.** Return to the shared room: “Katalos makes a personal media history tangible, explorable, and easy to share without exposing what you keep private.”

## Quick viewport check

- Desktop: confirm reading and TV nooks sit side by side and all covers can be opened with a keyboard.
- Phone width (under 768px): confirm the nooks and their status zones stack vertically, no horizontal page scroll appears, and controls remain comfortably tappable.
